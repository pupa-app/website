Why Pupa

Your agentic experience, made to last

So much of what you do with an agent is lost the moment the conversation ends — trapped in unstructured text, or scattered across the dozen services you stitch together. We keep pushing agents toward more structure, yet the results stay fragile and fragmented.

Pupa changes that. Whatever your setup — a single plain ChatGPT thread or an elaborate custom harness — your same agent now can, by default, use and build a real app alongside you. And that app stays with you: Pupa gives it a standard to persist, travel, and stay robust long after the chat is gone.

It fits either way: add Pupa on top of the agentic setup you already have, or make it the main app you use — with the agent just helping out.

And a Pupa app is just a JSON file that Pupa connects to your backend agent. Nothing is locked in, so it’s fully portable and works with any harness.

The portable standard

The .pupa bundle

A MyApp — its blocks, its memories, and its agent config — packs into a single portable file. The bundle ships the structure and intent, but no runtime: on import it runs on your host.

  • Inert JSON. Header plus the whole MyApp tree and its memories. No executable content — all rebuild logic lives in the open client, dispatched by component kind.
  • Hostile-validated import. Size caps, magic and version checks, a settings allow-list, memory-path guards, fresh IDs, and ref pruning. Tap-to-open shows a confirm sheet naming the app and its agent prompts before anything installs.
  • Portable experience, local execution. What travels is the app structure, personas, skills, and intent. What’s re-earned on the new host is tool availability, credentials, and private data.
  • No lock-in. Inspectable JSON, rebuilt by the open client, forward-compatible across app versions.
Contribute

Contribute code

Add new blocks — canvas components — to the app, or new agent hookups to the backend. Components are just SwiftUI views with a typed model — no agentic knowledge needed to build agent-compatible surfaces.

Open source

Everything is open, and swappable

The client, the backend, and the marketplace are all developed in the open. See the repos for each project’s terms.

pupa-app/pupa

The native iOS / macOS application. Ships SwiftUI canvas, it's components, Memories filesystem and how those are surfaced to your backend harness.

pupa-app/pupa-backend

The user-owned backend. Hooks-up on your Agent harness on your laptop (like Claude-Code). Run it locally next to the app or deploy your own in the cloud. Pair-once auth; your keys stay yours.

pupa-app/marketplace

A catalog of .pupa bundles you can browse and install from the app. Just an HTTPS index.json anyone can host. App content is CC0; tooling is MIT.
