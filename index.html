Pupa

Pupa turns an agentic experience into a persistent, typed app the agent works in with you — and that app is the portable unit.

A SwiftUI canvas that moulds into the shape you ask for — tracker, calendar, checklist, slack rooms, calculator, charts — driven by a side-panel chat, backed by a long-lived Memories filesystem, and speaking plain AG-UI to a backend you own.

Four ways in

Use, build, share, contribute

Pupa is one product with four doors. Live in a MyApp day-to-day, shape new ones, pass them to other people, or extend the open-source machinery underneath.

Use

Use a MyApp daily

Chat drives a typed canvas. Ask, and the surface moulds into a tracker, calendar, checklist, chart, or a room — the agent reads and writes it as first-class tools while you watch and edit the same surface.

Build

Build a MyApp

Mould the canvas into the shape you need — trackers, calendars, checklists, slack rooms, calculators, charts — cross-linked into one coherent app, backed by a long-lived Memories filesystem that survives sessions.

Share

Share a MyApp

Export any MyApp as an inert-JSON .pupa bundle and hand it to someone who runs it on their own host. Import and distribute through the marketplace — a plain HTTPS index anyone can host.

Contribute

Contribute code

Add new canvas components to the app or new agent hookups to the backend. Components are just SwiftUI views with a typed model — no agentic knowledge needed to build agent-compatible surfaces.

The portable standard

The .pupa bundle

A MyApp — its typed components, its memories, and its agent config — packs into a single portable file. The bundle ships the structure and intent, but no runtime: on import it runs on your host.

  • Inert JSON. Header plus the whole MyApp tree and its memories. No executable content — all rebuild logic lives in the open client, dispatched by component kind.
  • Hostile-validated import. Size caps, magic and version checks, a settings allow-list, memory-path guards, fresh IDs, and ref pruning. Tap-to-open shows a confirm sheet naming the app and its agent prompts before anything installs.
  • Portable experience, local execution. What travels is the app structure, personas, skills, and intent. What’s re-earned on the new host is tool availability, credentials, and private data. The bundle can’t act until you grant it your host’s capabilities.
  • No lock-in. Inspectable JSON, rebuilt by the open client, forward-compatible across app versions.

Open source

Everything is open, and swappable

The client, the backend, and the marketplace are all developed in the open. The AG-UI client ships as a standalone Swift package so anyone building an agent client on Apple platforms can reuse it without taking on Pupa’s UI. Licensing is being finalized — see the repos for each project’s terms.

pupa-app/pupa

The native iOS / macOS client — SwiftUI canvas, side-panel chat, Memories filesystem. Bundles AGUIKit, a standalone Swift AG-UI client package with no dependency on Pupa’s UI.

pupa-app/pupa-backend

The user-owned backend — FastAPI + LangGraph, speaking AG-UI over one SSE stream. Run it locally next to the app or deploy your own in the cloud. Pair-once auth; your keys stay yours.

pupa-app/marketplace

A catalog of .pupa bundles you can browse and install from the app — just an HTTPS index.json anyone can host. App content is CC0; tooling is MIT.
